About ChromeBooks

ChromeBooks is an online portal created to simulate the web-browsing experience of the 1990s. It features articles, blogs, images, and more. ChromeBooks was created on September 12th, 2014.

History of ChomeBooks

ChromeBooks was created in September 2014, and was updated throughout the rest of the month. It was developed using Adobe Dreamweaver CS5. The website didn't receive many updates for a while, but other sites were made such as the Network Neighborhood and the Neocities search engine, VHSearch.

The site was updated again in June 2015 with an updated, darker design when compared to the original.

On September 27th 2015, a few weeks after the 1 year anniversary of ChromeBooks, the site was mysteriously taken down for maintenance. During this time, the site was completely redesigned using Visual Studio Code, and all the code was rewritten by hand in order to clean up the mess left behind by Dreamweaver.

ChromeBooks 2.0 was launched on October 9th, 2015 with a more unified idea of what ChromeBooks should be - an online portal to Neocities.

Why the name?

ChromeBooks is designed as a web-appliance, as if there was a device whose singular purpose was browsing the web, such as a Google Chromebook. So, if Chromebooks existed in the 90s, this is what using what might be like.
